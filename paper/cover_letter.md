Dear Editors,

I am submitting the manuscript entitled "Acoustic drag suppression in periodic cellular solids from monopole cancellation and a kinematic gap" for consideration in Physical Review B.

The paper identifies a mechanism by which certain internal vibrations of foam cells propagate without losing energy to bulk acoustic phonons. Two geometric conditions are shown to be sufficient: (i) a force monopole selection rule $M_0 = 0$, enforced by centrosymmetry of the cell, and (ii) a kinematic gap that places the internal mode frequencies above the acoustic ceiling. When both hold, harmonic emission vanishes exactly at the zone center and is suppressed as $k^2$ at finite wavevector; Fermi golden rule estimates give anharmonic lifetimes of order $10^8$ oscillation periods at 1% strain. The results are verified on three foam lattices (C15 Laves, Kelvin BCC, Weaire--Phelan) with a reproducible test suite of 82 tests.

I believe the work is appropriate for PRB because it concerns lattice dynamics and phonon physics in periodic structures, and the protection mechanism---combining a symmetry selection rule with a spectral gap---may be relevant to vibration isolation in architected materials and mechanical metamaterials.
